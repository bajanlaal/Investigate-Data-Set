Introduction¶

For my project, I will be using the TMDb movie dataset

It contains the details like the budget, revenue, cast, director(s), etc for about 10,000 movies.

I. The questions that can be formulated and answered with the help of this dataset are:

What are the average popularities of movies according to budget levels? What are the profit trends of movies from year to year? What are the average runtimes of movies over the years? Which are the 5 cheapest and most expensive profitable movies of all time? Which variables affect the revenue and popularity of a film?

II. To find out which attributes lead to a successful/profitable movie the following information in terms of profitability needs to be extracted: Genre Actor/Actress Director Production Company Budget Runtime

    data = profit_df[column].str.cat(sep = '|')

    data = pd.Series(data.split('|'))
    count = data.value_counts(ascending = False)
    
    return count

Conclusions¶

From the above analysis, the following can be concluded as the criterias that need to be met in order to create a successful movie

  • The genre of the movie should be either Comedy, Drama, Action, Thriller or Adventure
  • Cast should include either Tom Crusie, Brad Pitt, Tom Hanks, Sylvester Stallone or Cameron Diaz
  • Director must be either Steven Spielberg, Robert Zemeckis, Clint Eastwood, Tim Burton or Ridley Scott
  • Production company must be either Universal Pictures, Warner Bros., Paramount Pictures, Twentieth Century Fox Film Corporation or Columbia Pictures
  • The budget of the movie must be around $60 million
  • The runtime of the movie must be close to 2 hours or 113 mintues to be exact.

The revenue should be around $255 million if all of the above criterias are met.

Limitations¶

  • The above conclusion is not a 100% formula to success as it only takes into considerations movies that have made a sizeable profit (50 million USD). Some movies may not have such a huge target as they canot get huge incestments so they settle for a much lower profit. There are a lot of cult movies too which may have earned revenue after it released in digital but not when they were out in theatres.
  • Also, the budget and revenue columns did not have a currency specified so there may be some differences due to fluctuating exchange rates. Different countries may also have different rules and regulations they have to follow which may restrict them from artistiic freedom.
  • Rows with NaN values were dropped, hence a lot of key data might have been lost in the process.
  • There are a lot of crew members apart from the director, cast and the production companies who help in creating a movie like writers, screenplay writers, editors, etc. but there was no data to analyse those aspects.
